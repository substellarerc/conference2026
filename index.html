SUBSTELLAR ASTROPHYSICS

Substellar science has emerged in the last few decades as new branch of Astrophysics that connects Stars, Exoplanets and the Solar System. The advent of new surveys such as Euclid and Rubin LSST is poised to increase the numbers of known substellar objects by more than an order of magnitude, while the James Webb Telescope is providing new details about their properties. This meeting will bring together researchers interested in Substellar science with special emphasis on exploiting the wealth of data provided by Euclid and complimenting it with other surveys and follow-up observations. A total eclipse of the Sun will take place during the conference.
